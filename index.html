"OK Computer" is the 3rd studio album by Radiohead which was released in 1997. It's an alternative rock album which has been cited by listeners, critics and musicians as one of the greatest albums of all time. The lyrics depict a world fraught with rampant consumerism, social alienation, emotional isolation and political malaise.

Kid A is the 4th studio album by Radiohead. After having suffered a mental breakdown promoting Radiohead's acclaimed 1997 album OK Computer, songwriter Thom Yorke envisioned a radical change in direction. The band replaced their rock sound with synthesisers, drum machines, the ondes Martenot, string orchestras and brass instruments, and incorporated influences from electronic music, krautrock, jazz, and 20th-century classical music.

"The Bends" is the 2nd studio album by Radiohead. It has been described as an alternative rock, Britpop, and indie rock album. The Bends marked the beginning of a shift in aesthetics and themes for the band, with greater use of keyboards and more abrasive guitar tracks, while balancing them with subtler ones.

"Amnesiac" is the 5th studio album by Radiohead. Recorded during the same sessions for the band's previous album Kid A, the album incorporates similar influences of electronic music, 20th century classical music, jazz and krautrock. Singer Thom Yorke described it as "another take on Kid A, a form of explanation.". Its lyrics and artwork explore themes influenced by memory and reincarnation, with influences from ancient Greek and Egyptian mythology.

"Hail to the Thief" is the 6th studio album by Radiohead. To avoid the protracted recording sessions of their previous albums, "Kid A" and "Amnesiac", Radiohead recorded "Hail to the Thief" quickly, focusing on recording live takes rather than overdubs. Songwriter Thom Yorke wrote many of the lyrics in response to the War on Terror and the resurgence of right-wing politics in the west at the turn of the millennium.
