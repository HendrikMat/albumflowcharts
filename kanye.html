Late Registration is the second album by Kanye West. Its production is notably more lush and elaborate than West's 2004 debut album The College Dropout, as he utilized intricate sampling methods and string orchestration. West's lyrics explore both personal and political themes, including poverty, drug trafficking, racism, healthcare, and the blood diamond trade.

Graduation is the third album by Kanye West. Inspired by indie rock bands and house music, Kanye developed a more anthemic style of rap music that would function well while he was performing in large arenas. He incorporated synthesizer sounds into his production and dabbled with electronic music, while sampling from a wider spectrum of music genres. Kanye dedicated much of Graduation towards analyzing himself and conveying his mixed view on his newfound fame.

The College Dropout is the debut studio album by Kanye West. On this album he used hise own "chipmunk soul" production style, which made use of sped-up, pitch shifted vocal samples from soul and R&B records, in addition to West's own drum programming and gospel choirs. Diverging from the then-dominant gangster persona in hip hop, West's lyrics concern themes of family, self-consciousness, materialism, religion, racism, and higher education.

My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy is the fifth album by Kanye West. Noted by critics for its maximalist aesthetic, opulent production quality, and dichotomous themes, My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy incorporates aspects of West's previous works, including soul, baroque, electro, and symphonic styles. The album deals with themes of excess and celebrity, and explores such issues as consumer culture, race, and the idealism of the American Dream.

808s & Heartbreak is the fourth album by Kanye West. Conceived in the wake of several distressing personal events, 808s & Heartbreak marked a major musical departure from West's previous rap records, instead featuring a sparse, electronic sound and West singing through an Auto-Tune vocal processor. His lyrics explore themes of loss and heartache, while the album's production abandons conventional hip hop sounds in favor of a minimalist sonic palette.
